## Introduction

Elastic Net Regression is a combination of Lasso Regression and Ridge Regression.[^1]
It combines the penalties of both Lasso and Ridge Regression to create a model that is more robust to outliers and multicollinearity.[^2]

[^1]: Originally proposed by Zou and Hastie in their 2005 paper *Regularization and Variable Selection via the Elastic Net*.
[^2]: Multicollinearity occurs when two or more predictors in a model are highly correlated, making coefficient estimates unstable.

## Mathematics Behind Elastic Net Regression

$$
\min _\beta\left( \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\hat{y}_i - y_i)^2 + \lambda_1 \sum_{j=1}^{p} |\theta_j| + \lambda_2 \sum_{j=1}^{p} |\theta_j^2| \right)
$$

where
1. $1/N \sum_{i=1}^{n} (\hat{y}_i - y_i)^2$ is the avg. Mean Squared Error.
2. $\lambda_1$ is the regularization parameter for Lasso penalty.
3. $\lambda_2$ is the regularization parameter for Ridge penalty.
4. $\theta_j$ is the coefficient of the $j^{th}$ feature.
4. $\lambda_1 \sum_{j=1}^{p} |\theta_j|$ is Lasso Penalty
5. $\lambda_2 \sum_{j=1}^{p} |\theta_j^2|$ is Ridge Penalty
